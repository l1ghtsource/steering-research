  \item \textbf{Intervention validity.} A frozen residual direction causally

  precede scale or sparse-feature claims.

\begin{frame}{The endpoint is a teacher-forced relative margin}
\begin{columns}[T,onlytextwidth]
\column{0.54\textwidth}
For prompt $x$ and fixed candidate completions $y_g$ and $y_b$:

\vspace{0.20cm}
\[
\ell(y\mid x)=\frac{1}{|y|}\sum_{t=1}^{|y|}
\log p_\theta(y_t\mid x,y_{<t})
\]
\[
m(x)=\ell(y_g\mid x)-\ell(y_b\mid x),
\qquad \Delta m=m_\alpha-m_0
\]

\begin{tightitemize}
  \item Positive $m$: the desirable answer has higher mean token log-probability.
  \item Positive $\Delta m$: steering improves this relative score.
\end{tightitemize}

\column{0.42\textwidth}
\claimbox{Green}{\textbf{What it controls}\newline Prompt, candidate texts, and length normalization are fixed across coefficients.}

\vspace{0.24cm}
\claimbox{Amber}{\textbf{What it does not control}\newline Generator identity, answer style, format, semantics, and sequence-level selection probability.}
\end{columns}

\source{Repository convention: undesirable is the positive contrast class; evaluation margin is desirable minus undesirable.}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{Appendix: research abstract}
\small
This project studies whether residual-stream interventions can alter
behavior-related preference scores in Qwen3.5. Dense directions are estimated
from LatentBehaviorBench contrast pairs and injected into a frozen
Qwen3.5-2B-Base model. The current endpoint is a length-normalized,
teacher-forced log-probability margin between fixed desirable and undesirable
answers to the same prompt.

\vspace{0.30cm}
On an internal ordered 30\% extraction partition, refusal and hallucination
margins move consistently under selected residual interventions. For refusal,
raw $\alpha=-4$ at layer 12 yields mean $\Delta m=+0.8161$ and changes pair
accuracy from 0.433 to 0.833; however, the intervention is 96.22\% of the mean
residual norm and the answer pairs are confounded by source model, style, and
length. For hallucination, raw $\alpha=+4$ at layer 23 yields
$\Delta m=+0.3248$ with a near-linear coefficient response, while answer format
and length remain strongly confounded. The unsafe-planning series repeats one
answer pair and is treated only as a diagnostic.

\vspace{0.30cm}
The present evidence therefore establishes a causal effect on fixed-pair scores,
not clean behavioral control. The next stage introduces grouped splits,
construct-matched pairs, frozen norm-scaled interventions, causal controls, and
open-generation evaluation before any scale or sparse-feature claim.
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}{Appendix: direction and intervention}
\small
For layer $\ell$, answer-token residual activations are averaged within each
example and then across the direction subset:

\[
\mathbf{d}_{\ell}=
\frac{\mu^{\text{undesirable}}_{\ell}-
\mu^{\text{desirable}}_{\ell}}
{\left\|\mu^{\text{undesirable}}_{\ell}-
\mu^{\text{desirable}}_{\ell}\right\|_2}.
\]

At inference, the same vector is added at selected token positions:

\[
\mathbf{h}^{\prime}_{\ell,t}=\mathbf{h}_{\ell,t}+\alpha\mathbf{d}_{\ell}.
\]

\column{0.48\textwidth}
\begin{tightitemize}
  \item Frozen Qwen3.5-2B-Base parameters
  \item 24 layers, hidden size 2048
  \item Assistant-answer mean activation view
\end{tightitemize}
\column{0.48\textwidth}
\begin{tightitemize}
  \item E016 uses raw coefficients
  \item E017 scales coefficients by mean residual norm
  \item Current text serialization is repository-defined, not an official chat template
\end{tightitemize}
\end{columns}
\end{frame}
